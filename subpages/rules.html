The cards

There are 78 cards in a Tarot deck.
There are 5 suits.the 4 we are use to (♠,♥,♦,♣) + another suits named the Atouts.
The regular suits include 1 - 10, the jack, knight, queen, and king.
The Atouts include 1-21 as well as the excuse. Example of atouts below.

Dealing

Card distribution
Number of Players Cards per Player Dog
3 Players 24 cards 6 cards
4 Players 18 cards 6 cards
5 Players 15 cards 3 cards
The dealer deals in packs of 3 counter clockwise and may choose to place cards in the dog anytime except for the last card

The contracts

Starting with the player left of the dealer and going counter-clockwise, the players assess their hand and either bids for a contract or passes. A player must bid on a higher contract than any player before. If no one bids on a contract the redeal the cards.

  1. Petite (x1)

    Everyone sees the dog. The taker incorperates the dog into their hand but they have to discard the same number of cards as the dog. The discard cards goes in the takers win pile face down.

  2. Garde (x2)

    Everyone sees the dog. The taker incorperates the dog into their hand but they have to discard the same number of cards as the dog. The discard cards goes in the takers win pile face down.

  3. Garde Sans (x3)

    The dog goes straight into the takers win pile face down. No one sees the dog.

  4. Garde Contre (x4)

    The dog goes straight into the defense win pile face down. No one sees the dog.

Play

The person left of the dealer starts the first round.
For each round, the players each play 1 card face up and they go in order, counter-clockwise.
Every player must play the suit that started the round if they can. If they do not have the requested suit, they must play an Atout if they can.
If a player plays an Atout, they must play an Atout higher than any other Atout played in that round, if possible.
If the player does not have the requested suit or atouts, then they may play anything.
The excuse may be played anytime.


If an atout is played(besides excuse) the highest atout wins the round and the winner takes all the cards in the round into their win pile (face down).
If an atout is not played, the highest card in the suit that was requested (first suit played in the round) wins.
If an excuse is played. The excuse is given back to the person who played it to put in their win pile, regardless of who won the round.
The player who won the round starts the next round.
Play till all cards have been played.
